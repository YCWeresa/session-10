2017 was an astonishing year for the Chinese mobile payment companies, mainly Alibaba who runs AliPay and Tencent who developed WeChat Pay. In 2017, $15.4 trillion worth of mobile payments were handled by third-party platforms in China—more than 40 times the amount processed in the US. Alipay, owned by Alibaba, handled nearly 54 percent of those payments, and WeChat Pay, owned by Tencent, processed around 39 percent. Together, Alipay and WeChat Pay make up for 92% of the global mobile payment market.
